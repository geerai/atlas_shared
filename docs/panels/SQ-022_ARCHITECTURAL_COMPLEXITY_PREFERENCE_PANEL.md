# Panel Dossier: SQ-022

## Question

What levels of architectural visual complexity do people prefer, and how does preference relate to environmental familiarity or cultural background?

## Topic / Subtopic

Spatial Complexity / Aesthetic preference

## Status

`llm_panel_drafted`

## In-Criteria (Central Cases)

1. The study measures human aesthetic preference, beauty judgment, or liking responses to architectural scenes or building facades varying in visual complexity.
2. The study manipulates or measures visual complexity using quantitative methods (fractal dimension, edge density, information entropy, Shannon entropy, Fourier analysis, feature congestion) and relates this to preference.
3. The study tests the Berlyne inverted-U hypothesis — that moderate complexity is preferred over both low and high complexity — in architectural or built environment stimuli.
4. The study tests whether familiarity, expertise, or cultural background moderates the complexity-preference relationship.

## Out-Criteria (Clear Rejections)

1. Papers about visual complexity in art, music, or abstract patterns without architectural stimuli. General experimental aesthetics belongs to psychology, not SQ-022.
2. Papers about architectural complexity as a design philosophy (postmodernism, deconstructivism) without empirical preference measurement.
3. Papers about spatial complexity for wayfinding (corridor configuration, floor plan). These are SQ-021 territory.
4. Papers about information complexity in web design or UX without built environment context.

## Hard Cases (Borderline Decisions)

1. **Studies using photographs of real buildings without quantified complexity**: Many preference studies show building photographs but describe complexity only qualitatively ("simple" vs. "complex"). *Tend toward: include if the study uses more than two complexity levels and the stimuli are clearly architectural; edge case if only a simple dichotomy is used.*

2. **Studies of facade detail preference**: Research on ornamentation, decoration, or detail level in building facades. *Tend toward: include — ornamentation is a primary dimension of architectural visual complexity.*

3. **Eye-tracking studies of architectural scenes**: Gaze pattern analysis showing where people look in complex vs. simple buildings. *Tend toward: include if the study connects viewing behavior to preference or aesthetic judgment, not just visual search.*

4. **Cross-cultural studies of general visual complexity preference** using non-architectural stimuli. *Tend toward: edge case — the cultural moderation finding transfers, but the stimuli are not architectural.*

5. **Studies of architectural typicality and preference**: Research on prototypicality (how typical a building looks) rather than complexity per se. Typicality and complexity are related but distinct. *Tend toward: include if the study discusses both or positions prototypicality as a confound with complexity.*

## False Friends

- **"Complexity"** in organizational theory — management complexity
- **"Visual complexity"** in computer vision — rendering algorithms
- **"Architectural complexity"** in software — system design patterns
- **"Preference"** in economics — consumer choice
- **"Cultural background"** in diversity studies without environmental perception

## Evidence Markers

- Berlyne's collative variables: complexity, novelty, incongruity, surprisingness
- Quantified complexity: fractal dimension, edge density, JPEG file size, perimetric complexity
- Preference measures: Likert scales, paired comparisons, forced-choice preference
- Familiarity ratings, expertise classification (architect vs. non-architect)
- Cross-cultural comparisons: at least two cultural groups
- References to Berlyne (1971), Wohlwill (1976), Stamps (2004), Vartanian et al. (2013, 2015)

## Escalation Triggers

The following cases MUST be escalated to a subscribed LLM adjudicator rather than resolved by keyword heuristics:

1. **Studies finding a linear (not inverted-U) relationship** — some evidence suggests that experts prefer higher complexity without an optimum. The adjudicator must determine whether the study sample includes architectural expertise as a variable, and assess whether the linear finding challenges Berlyne or merely reflects expertise moderation.

2. **Studies using computational image analysis (CNNs, image entropy)** to predict architectural preference. The adjudicator must assess whether the computational metric is validated against human judgment, and determine whether the CNN features subsume simpler metrics like edge density or fractal dimension.

3. **Building-type moderation**: Preference for complexity may differ between residential, commercial, civic, and sacred architecture. The adjudicator must flag building-type as a potential moderator and determine whether the study tests this interaction or assumes homogeneity across building types.

## Unresolved Ambiguities

- "Visual complexity" encompasses multiple dimensions (number of elements, variety of elements, irregularity, ornamentation, color variation). Different operationalizations may yield different preference curves.
- Whether the inverted-U is a genuine psychological function or an artifact of averaging across individuals with different optimal points is debated.
