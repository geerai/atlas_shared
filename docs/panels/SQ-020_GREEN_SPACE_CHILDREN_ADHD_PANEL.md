# Panel Dossier: SQ-020

## Question

How does access to green space affect cognitive development and sustained attention in children, particularly those diagnosed with ADHD?

## Topic / Subtopic

Urban Green Space / Children and ADHD

## Status

`llm_panel_drafted`

## In-Criteria (Central Cases)

1. The study measures the relationship between green space access (residential, school, neighborhood) and cognitive development or sustained attention in children (ages 3–18).
2. The study specifically tests green space effects on children diagnosed with or screened for ADHD, measuring symptom severity, attention performance, or executive function after nature exposure.
3. The study uses an experimental or quasi-experimental design where nature exposure is the intervention and an attention or cognitive development measure is the outcome in a pediatric sample.
4. Epidemiological studies linking childhood green space exposure to neurodevelopmental outcomes (working memory, attention, academic performance) at the population level.

## Out-Criteria (Clear Rejections)

1. Papers about adult attention restoration after nature exposure. These belong to SQ-001.
2. Papers about ADHD pharmacotherapy or behavioral intervention without environmental or nature component.
3. Papers about children's physical activity in green spaces without cognitive or attention outcomes.
4. Papers about green space and children's respiratory health, allergies, or physical health without cognitive/attention measures.

## Hard Cases (Borderline Decisions)

1. **Schoolyard greening intervention studies**: Studies measuring academic performance (test scores, grades) after schoolyard greening. *Tend toward: include — academic performance in children plausibly reflects attention and cognitive development, though confounds exist.*

2. **Nature-based ADHD interventions (adventure therapy, therapeutic gardening)**: These involve nature exposure but with additional therapeutic structure. *Tend toward: include if attention is measured; edge case if only behavioral or emotional outcomes are reported.*

3. **Air pollution mediation studies**: Green space near schools reducing pollution exposure, which in turn affects neurodevelopment. *Tend toward: include — the mechanism (air quality vs. visual restoration vs. physical activity) is informative for understanding the causal pathway.*

4. **Pre-school or infant studies (ages 0-3)**: Nature contact and early cognitive milestones. The cognitive measures are very different from school-age attention. *Tend toward: edge case — include if the study uses a validated developmental assessment.*

5. **Forest school and outdoor education studies**: Children in forest kindergartens vs. indoor classrooms. *Tend toward: include if cognitive/attention outcomes are measured; reject if only social-emotional or motor outcomes are reported.*

## False Friends

- **"Green space"** and children's physical fitness — motor development, not cognition
- **"Attention"** in educational psychology — teacher attention to students, not child attentional capacity
- **"ADHD"** treatment studies without environmental component
- **"Child development"** broadly — social, emotional, motor development without cognitive/attention focus
- **"Nature play"** advocacy — opinion pieces without empirical data

## Evidence Markers

- ADHD diagnosis or screening instrument (Conners, SNAP-IV, CBCL attention subscale)
- Attention tasks: CPT, ANT, CANTAB, digit span, Trail Making Test
- Green space exposure: NDVI buffer around home/school, park access, nature contact diary
- Sample: children or adolescents, age clearly specified
- Longitudinal or repeated-measures designs
- References to Faber Taylor & Kuo (2009, 2011), Dadvand et al. (2015, 2017), Wells (2000)

## Escalation Triggers

The following cases MUST be escalated to a subscribed LLM adjudicator rather than resolved by keyword heuristics:

1. **Studies finding that the ADHD-nature benefit is explained entirely by physical activity** (children run more outdoors), not by attentional restoration. The adjudicator must assess whether the mediation analysis is statistically adequate (formal test of indirect effect, not just inclusion of a covariate) and report whether the ART-specific mechanism survives the mediation test.

2. **Studies comparing green schoolyard effects across SES strata** — do low-SES children benefit more, creating an equity dimension? The adjudicator must determine whether the study has sufficient statistical power for the interaction test and flag the equity implication for design policy.

3. **Studies with objective neurodevelopmental measures** (fMRI, EEG, neuropsychological batteries) rather than parent/teacher rating scales. The adjudicator must prioritize these for review as they provide stronger evidence for biological mechanisms than subjective ratings, and cross-reference the neuroimaging findings with SQ-001 (ART neural evidence).

## Unresolved Ambiguities

- Is the mechanism in ADHD-nature studies attention restoration (ART) or stimulation reduction (lower demand on effortful inhibition)?
- Self-selection: do families with ADHD children strategically seek green neighborhoods? Few studies address this.
- Clinical significance: statistical improvements in attention scores may not reach clinically meaningful thresholds.

## LLM Drafting Note

This constitution was drafted with LLM panel reasoning. SQ-020 combines two strong sub-literatures (green space and child cognition, nature and ADHD) that use different methods and standards. The constitution bridges both while maintaining the attention/cognition criterion. Including Dadvand (epidemiological exposure assessment) alongside Faber Taylor & Kuo (experimental ART) ensures methodological range.
