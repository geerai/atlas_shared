# Panel Dossier: SQ-014

## Question

What is the evidence that natural sounds (birdsong, flowing water) reduce stress or improve cognitive performance compared to urban noise?

## Topic / Subtopic

Acoustic Environment / Nature sounds

## In-Criteria (Central Cases)

1. The study exposes human participants to natural sounds (birdsong, water sounds, wind in trees, rain, ocean waves, insect sounds) and measures stress reduction, cognitive performance, or restorative outcomes.
2. The study compares natural sounds to urban or anthropogenic noise (traffic, construction, machinery, speech) using the same outcome measures.
3. The study examines soundscape composition — the ratio or interaction of natural to anthropogenic sounds — and its effect on psychological or physiological states.
4. Meta-analyses or systematic reviews of the restorative effects of natural vs. urban soundscapes.

## Out-Criteria (Clear Rejections)

1. Papers about noise pollution and hearing damage from industrial or traffic noise without any natural sound condition.
2. Papers about music therapy or white noise for sleep without natural sound stimuli.
3. Papers about bioacoustics (animal communication, acoustic ecology) that study animal behavior rather than human psychological response.
4. Papers about noise mapping, sound measurement methodology, or acoustic engineering without human outcomes.

## Hard Cases (Borderline Decisions)

1. **Soundscape studies measuring "pleasantness" or "preference" but not stress or cognition**: Axelsson's model defines soundscape quality on pleasantness-eventfulness dimensions. A finding that birdsong increases pleasantness is relevant but does not directly measure stress or cognitive performance. *Tend toward: include if pleasantness is interpreted as restoration or wellbeing-related; edge case if it is treated as a purely acoustic descriptor.*

2. **Nature-and-music combinations**: Studies playing nature sounds mixed with ambient music or soundtracking birdsong over a musical piece. *Tend toward: edge case — the natural sound contribution cannot be isolated from the music.*

3. **Pink noise or brown noise studies**: These spectral noise types resemble some natural sounds (waterfall ≈ pink noise). *Tend toward: reject unless the study explicitly frames the noise type as a nature proxy or tests the "naturalness" of the spectral distribution.*

4. **ASMR studies using nature-trigger sounds**: Whispering rain, crackling fire. These use nature-associated sounds for autonomous sensory meridian response. *Tend toward: edge case — the mechanism (ASMR tingles) is distinct from restorative environments.*

5. **Outdoor studies where the soundscape is not the independent variable**: Field studies measuring wellbeing during nature walks where sounds are part of the experience but not isolated. *Tend toward: edge case — the sound contribution cannot be separated from visual, olfactory, and movement components.*

## False Friends

- **"Natural frequency"** — engineering resonance, not nature sounds
- **"Sound environment"** in industrial safety — occupational noise limits
- **"Water sound"** in plumbing — pipe noise
- **"Bird call"** in telecommunications — voice connection test
- **"Acoustic ecology"** in conservation biology — biodiversity monitoring

## Evidence Markers

- Specific sound types named: birdsong, water flow, wind, rain, ocean, crickets
- dB(A) levels of stimulus calibration
- Physiological measures: HRV, skin conductance, cortisol, blood pressure after sound exposure
- Cognitive tasks: attention, working memory, reaction time during or after sound
- Subjective measures: PRS, restoration scale, perceived stress (PSS), mood (POMS)
- References to Ratcliffe et al. (2013, 2016), Alvarsson et al. (2010), Gould van Praag et al. (2017)

## Escalation Triggers

The following cases MUST be escalated to a subscribed LLM adjudicator rather than resolved by keyword heuristics:

1. **Papers bundling natural sounds with visual nature exposure** where the auditory contribution cannot be isolated. The adjudicator must determine whether the study design allows separation of auditory and visual channels — if the methods section describes audio-only conditions, accept; if nature sounds are always paired with nature images, classify as edge case for SQ-014 and cross-reference SQ-001/SQ-004.

2. **Studies of acoustic ecology measuring soundscape quality indices** (L_eq, sound pressure level, frequency spectrum) in natural environments without psychological outcome measurement. The adjudicator must determine whether the acoustic characterization provides sufficient methodological foundation to warrant inclusion as contextual evidence.

3. **Studies of threatening natural sounds** (thunderstorms, animal growls, insect swarms) that produce stress rather than restoration. The adjudicator must determine whether these count as counter-evidence to the nature-sounds-are-restorative claim (and should be included) or whether they test a different question.

## Unresolved Ambiguities

- Is the restorative effect of natural sounds about the information content (signals of safety, biodiversity) or the spectral properties (1/f distribution, absence of abrupt transients)?
- Duration thresholds for sound-based restoration are poorly established — is a 5-minute birdsong clip sufficient?

## LLM Drafting Note

This constitution was drafted with LLM panel reasoning. SQ-014 sits at the intersection of soundscape ecology, environmental psychology, and acoustic engineering. The main challenge is distinguishing studies that measure soundscape quality from those that measure psychological restoration — the former is necessary but not sufficient for the latter.
