# Panel Dossier: SQ-025

## Question

How well do post-occupancy evaluation (POE) studies control for self-selection bias and social desirability when measuring wellbeing in biophilic buildings?

## Topic / Subtopic

Post-Occupancy Evaluation / Method quality

## In-Criteria (Central Cases)

1. The study is a post-occupancy evaluation of a building with biophilic design features that measures occupant wellbeing, and the study report or methodology section explicitly discusses self-selection bias, social desirability, or response bias as limitations or controlled-for confounds.
2. The study compares POE results from biophilic buildings to matched comparison buildings, controlling for occupant demographic or psychographic differences.
3. Methodological or meta-scientific papers that evaluate the quality of the POE evidence base for biophilic design claims — specifically examining whether observed wellbeing differences could be explained by selection or reporting bias.
4. Natural experiment or relocation studies where the same occupants are measured before and after moving to a biophilic building, allowing within-subject comparison.

## Out-Criteria (Clear Rejections)

1. Papers about POE methodology for energy, HVAC, or building system performance without occupant wellbeing.
2. Papers about biophilic design advocacy or case study descriptions without formal POE data collection.
3. Papers about workplace satisfaction in general without specific biophilic design features.
4. Papers about survey methodology in psychology without built environment application.

## Hard Cases (Borderline Decisions)

1. **POE studies of biophilic buildings that do NOT discuss self-selection**: Many biophilic building evaluations report positive outcomes without acknowledging selection bias. *Tend toward: include as evidence of methodological gaps — these papers are relevant precisely because they illustrate the problem SQ-025 asks about.*

2. **IEQ multi-factor studies**: POEs measuring biophilic features alongside thermal, acoustic, and lighting satisfaction. *Tend toward: include if biophilic features can be identified in the results; edge case if only composite IEQ scores are reported.*

3. **Green building certification studies (LEED, WELL, BREEAM)**: These evaluate certified buildings but may not isolate biophilic features from other certification requirements. *Tend toward: edge case — include if biophilic features are explicitly attributed.*

4. **Employee productivity studies in newly renovated offices**: The Hawthorne effect (novelty of any change) confounds the biophilic attribution. *Tend toward: include — the Hawthorne/novelty confound is itself methodologically informative.*

5. **Pre-/post-renovation studies without control group**: Same occupants, same building, biophilic features added. *Tend toward: include — this is the most common design, and its limitations are part of what SQ-025 investigates.*

## False Friends

- **"Post-occupancy"** in clinical literature — post-operative occupancy of hospital beds
- **"Selection bias"** in genetics — natural selection
- **"Social desirability"** in survey design — general survey methodology without building context
- **"Biophilic"** in conservation — biophilia as environmental ethic
- **"Building performance"** — energy, structural, or fire performance

## Evidence Markers

- Named POE instruments: BUS, CBE, OLS, BOSSA, Leesman
- Self-selection discussion: "occupants may differ," "self-selection," "endogeneity"
- Response bias discussion: "social desirability," "demand characteristics," "expectation effects"
- Matched comparison or control building
- Pre-/post-move design with identified occupant panel
- References to Leaman et al., Brager & de Dear, Vischer, Browning et al. (2014)

## Escalation Triggers

The following cases MUST be escalated to a subscribed LLM adjudicator rather than resolved by keyword heuristics:

1. **Papers explicitly demonstrating that biophilic POE results change after controlling for self-selection** — i.e., the biophilic effect is reduced or eliminated. The adjudicator must assess the credibility of the selection-control method and determine whether the biophilic benefit survives adjustment. These are the highest-value findings.

2. **Registered reports or pre-registered POE studies** — these represent improved methodology applying to biophilic buildings. The adjudicator must verify that the pre-registration was completed before data collection and flag the study as methodologically superior to standard POEs.

3. **Longitudinal POEs** tracking the same building over multiple years to see whether initial positive effects persist or represent a novelty response. The adjudicator must determine the follow-up duration and assess whether any observed decline in satisfaction is consistent with the Hawthorne effect or with genuine adaptation.

## Unresolved Ambiguities

- In most biophilic building evaluations, occupants are not randomly assigned — they chose to work there, were assigned by management, or were the population before renovation. Self-selection is nearly universal but rarely controlled.
- Social desirability is especially problematic when occupants know the building was designed to be "healthy" or "green" — expectation effects may inflate satisfaction ratings.
- The definition of "biophilic building" is not standardized — a building may have a single living wall or a comprehensive biophilic design program.

## LLM Drafting Note

This constitution was drafted with LLM panel reasoning. SQ-025 is unique in the bank because it is primarily a methodological quality question rather than a substantive evidence question. The constitution must include both high-quality POEs (which address bias) and low-quality POEs (which illustrate the problem). The panel pairs Leaman and Brager (POE methodology pioneers with institutional credibility) with Candido (modern IEQ measurement), Hua (biophilic workplace evaluation), and Williams (certification and evidence standards) to ensure both methodological rigor and awareness of how POE results are used in building certification.
