# Panel Dossier: SQ-027

## Question

Do indoor plants measurably improve air quality or cognitive performance, or do effects stem primarily from visual and psychological pathways?

## Topic / Subtopic

Indoor Air Quality / Plants and cognition

## In-Criteria (Central Cases)

1. The study tests the effect of indoor plants on air quality (VOC removal, CO₂ absorption, particulate matter reduction) AND/OR on cognitive performance, stress, or wellbeing in a controlled or field setting.
2. The study attempts to separate the visual/psychological pathway (seeing plants → mood → performance) from the air-quality pathway (plants filtering pollutants → better air → better cognition).
3. The study compares rooms with live plants to rooms with no plants, artificial plants, or photographs of plants to isolate the mechanism.
4. Meta-analyses or systematic reviews evaluating the overall evidence for indoor-plant effects and the relative contribution of air-quality vs. psychological pathways.

## Out-Criteria (Clear Rejections)

1. Papers about outdoor urban greening, street trees, or parks. Indoor plants are a distinct intervention.
2. Papers about agricultural greenhouses or horticultural production.
3. Papers about phytoremediation of contaminated soil or water using plants — environmental engineering, not indoor air.
4. Papers about gardening therapy or horticultural therapy where the activity (gardening) is the intervention, not passive plant presence.

## Hard Cases (Borderline Decisions)

1. **NASA clean air study (Wolverton 1989)**: The foundational claim that plants remove indoor pollutants. Recent critiques (Cummings & Waring 2019) show that the NASA results used sealed chambers and do not scale to real buildings. *Tend toward: include both the original study and the critique — this is the central evidence dispute.*

2. **Green wall / living wall studies**: Large-scale plant installations covering entire walls. These may have meaningful air-quality effects at scale, unlike individual potted plants. *Tend toward: include — green walls represent a design-relevant scale of indoor vegetation.*

3. **Artificial plant studies**: Comparing real vs. artificial plants for psychological outcomes. If artificial plants provide the same benefit, the mechanism is visual, not air-quality. *Tend toward: definitely include — this is the key experiment for separating pathways.*

4. **Studies of plant care as activity**: Does watering and maintaining plants provide engagement, responsibility, and psychological benefit beyond the visual presence of the plant? *Tend toward: edge case — the activity mechanism is distinct from both air quality and visual pathways.*

5. **Microbial diversity arguments**: Recent research suggesting indoor plants increase beneficial microbial diversity in building air, which may affect occupant immune function. *Tend toward: include — this is a third pathway (not air filtering, not visual) that is scientifically emerging.*

## False Friends

- **"Plant" in manufacturing** — industrial facility
- **"Indoor environment quality"** (IEQ) without plant component — HVAC optimization
- **"Green building"** — sustainability certification, not indoor vegetation
- **"Cognitive enhancement"** with supplements or nootropics — pharmacology
- **"Air quality"** focused on PM2.5 and outdoor pollution infiltration — mechanical ventilation

## Evidence Markers

- VOC measurements: formaldehyde, benzene, toluene, xylene concentrations (ppm/ppb)
- CO₂ levels: ppm before/after plant introduction
- Cognitive tasks: attention, typing speed, color-word Stroop, mathematical performance
- Physiological measures: blood pressure, skin conductance, cortisol
- Live vs. artificial vs. photograph/image plant conditions
- References to Wolverton (1989), Bringslimark et al. (2009), Lohr et al. (1996), Cummings & Waring (2019), Lee et al. (2015)

## Escalation Triggers

The following cases MUST be escalated to a subscribed LLM adjudicator rather than resolved by keyword heuristics:

1. **Direct refutations of the NASA clean air claim** (Cummings & Waring 2019 calculated that you would need 10-1000 plants per square meter to match HVAC air exchange). The adjudicator must assess whether the refutation's ventilation assumptions match realistic office conditions, and determine whether the practical recommendation changes fundamentally (plants for visual benefit only, not air quality).

2. **Studies of biophilic design where plants are bundled with other features**. The plant contribution cannot be isolated. The adjudicator must determine whether the plant component is the primary design feature or incidental, and flag for SQ-008 cross-reference if biophilic design is the broader framing.

3. **Studies of plant scent (volatile organic compound emissions from plants)** — some indoor plants emit pleasant terpenes that may have independent mood effects, complicating the mechanism story. The adjudicator must determine whether the scent pathway is controlled for in the study design and assess whether this represents a third mechanism beyond air-quality and visual pathways.

## Unresolved Ambiguities

- The practical air-quality benefit of indoor plants at realistic densities appears to be negligible compared to mechanical ventilation. The scientific consensus has shifted against the air-quality pathway, but the popular belief persists.
- Whether the visual pathway operates through biophilia (innate preference for vegetation), processing fluency (green is calming), or attention restoration (soft fascination) is not settled.

## LLM Drafting Note

This constitution was drafted with LLM panel reasoning. SQ-027 is distinctive because the question directly pits two competing explanations against each other. The inclusion of Wolverton (NASA study originator, but his 1989 findings are now contested) and Waring (whose 2019 paper essentially demolished the air-quality claim at practical densities) ensures the constitution captures the full evidence arc from initial claim through rigorous critique.
