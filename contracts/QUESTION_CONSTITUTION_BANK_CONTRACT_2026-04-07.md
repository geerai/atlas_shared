# Question Constitution Bank Contract

This bank is the machine-usable form of a panel's judgment about a research question.

It is not merely a bag of search hints. Each question entry must specify:

- what counts as in-bounds
- what counts as out-of-bounds
- what counts as hard-case or adjacent
- which article types are acceptable, marginal, or excluded
- what evidence markers matter for stronger acceptance

## Why This Exists

The live question banks in Atlas currently contain `question_text`, `topic`, `subtopic`, and `search_hints`, but they do not contain formal inclusion, exclusion, or edge-case criteria. That means a question can guide search but cannot yet govern a serious intake filter.

This bank supplies that missing constitutional layer.

The Markdown panel dossiers should be kept. They are the human deliberative record.
The JSON constitutions are operational derivatives for software use.

## Required Method

Each constitution should be created in three stages:

1. `llm_panel_drafted`
   An LLM produces an initial constitution draft from the source question, search hints, and domain framing.

2. `panel_reviewed`
   A small expert panel revises the draft, especially the boundary cases and false friends.

3. `panel_confirmed`
   A curator or instructor signs off on the final version for operational use.

In short: LLM first, human review second, operational adoption third.

## Operational Rule

The filter may apply heuristics directly, but constitutions should not be generated by heuristics alone. A pure Python expansion from `search_hints` is not epistemically good enough.
